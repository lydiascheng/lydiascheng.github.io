Applied AI Design Jam

2 hour compeition to come up with a deliverable for a given problem statement

given background

Applied AI has been contracted to analyze college football viewership and ticket sales. How can they increase college football viewership (in-person and on tv/internet)?

We had been provided with information about the viewership from 2019, as well as 2020’s new recruits.

OBJECTIVE

Develop a DATA-DRIVEN SOLUTION that will engage new users / viewers. Their solution should include data analysis, strong design thinking, and engineering architecture within the 45 minute time constraint.

OUR APPROACH

My team focused on trying to maintain the current college football audience will also focusing on gaining viewership given the context of COVID-19.

personas

By initially identifying our two main target audiences, we made personas based on each of them to gain a better understanding of their wants, pain points, etc.

ultimate fan frank

“I miss being able to talk about the games with my friends, it’s hard to do that over Facetime while the game going on in the background.”

social sara

“Honestly, I have no idea how football works. I just like to tailgate with all my friends.”

features

After creating a better understanding of our intended audience, the team focused on generating features that would address the needs and wants of both user groups.

trivia feature

For the already existing college football superfan, we created a trivia feature to be played during ad breaks or just during their free time by integrating the data we were given.

Further incentives for the superfan to appreciate this feature include a leadership board to compete with their other superfan friends. Additionally, by playing trivia, users can win prizes such as college football merchandise.

chat feature

For the users that only go to football games for the social experience, we developed a chat feature to recreate the social aspect of going to an in person college football game.

Users can choose to chat with preexisting friends or to be matched with strangers during each advertisement break.

OUTCOME

After the 45 minutes lasped, the team presented our final deliverable, answered questions from the judging panel, and won 1st place in the competition.
